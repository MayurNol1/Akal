Description expand_more

The Panchmukhi (5-faced) Rudraksha Mala is one of the most revered and widely used malas in Hindu spiritual practice. Each bead carries five distinct faces (mukhis), believed to represent the five elements and the five aspects of Lord Shiva.

This 108-bead mala is hand-strung on traditional red thread by artisans in Haridwar, using seeds directly sourced from Nepal's high-altitude forests — the only region where genuine Panchmukhi seeds grow.

Specifications expand_more
  • Bead size: 6–7 mm (standard), 8–9 mm (large)
  • Bead count: 108 + 1 guru bead
  • Thread: Natural red cotton (traditional) or silk
  • Origin: Nepal (Himalayan range, above 1,400m)
  • Certification: GIA-equivalent Rudraksha Testing
  • Energisation: Prana Pratishtha ritual performed before dispatch
Spiritual Significance expand_more

The 5-faced Rudraksha is associated with Lord Shiva in his Kalagni Rudra form. It is said to bring inner peace, mental clarity, and protection from negative influences. Regular use is believed to balance the Pancha Pranas and enhance meditative depth.

Care Instructions expand_more
  • Anoint with sesame or coconut oil monthly
  • Avoid water immersion for extended periods
  • Store in a clean cloth pouch when not in use
  • Do not wear during sleep initially — allow the energies to harmonise gradually
